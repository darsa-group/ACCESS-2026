Financial clarifications

The summer school is sponsored by the InsectAI COST Action, and reimbursements follows the rules of the COST Association. There are no special provisions for training courses, so reimbursement follows the general event attendance rules.

This means that participants must initially cover their own expenses and submit a reimbursement claim after the course has ended. Unfortunately, due to administrative constraints, we cannot directly pay for travel or accommodation.

Reimbursement consists of two components: travel costs and a daily allowance.

For the travel costs, participants must submit receipts for travel exceeding 50 km. The reimbursable amount corresponds to the exact documented cost. Please note that only direct and uninterrupted travel can be reimbursed. If a stop longer than 24 hours is made on the way to the event, only one leg of the journey may be reimbursed under COST rules.Additionally, cumulative tickets (e.g. Interrail) are not claimable, not even in part. Travel costs should not exceed 500 EUR. For selected candidates travelling from outside mainland Europe & UK, we will be in touch separately.

The daily allowance is a fixed per-diem amount intended to cover accommodation, meals, and local travel (<50 km). It is possible that participants receive an invoice from the venue for these costs shortly before the course begins. The daily allowance will be sufficient to cover the venue invoice as well as additional costs.

All reimbursements are made in Euros, irrespective of the currency of the expenses.

You can find more details on the Annotated rules for COST Actions document (version v3.0, Sep 2025 when consulted), specifically section A1-3.1 Travel reimbursement rules.

To illustrate how the process typically works: a candidate applies to ACCESS before 15 April and receives an offer to attend on 20 May. After accepting the offer, she books her travel to Aarhus, paying approximately 350 EUR for a return ticket. Shortly before the course (around 15 August), she receives an invoice for accommodation and meals of 5,600 DKK (~750 EUR), which she pays directly to the venue. During the course, participants receive guidance on how to complete their reimbursement claim. After ACCESS concludes, she submits the claim and receives the full reimbursement of 1,100 EUR within 1–4 weeks.

In previous years, some participants arranged for their institution or research grant to temporarily cover travel and accommodation costs. After receiving the reimbursement, they returned the funds to their institution or grant. The exact procedure depends on the institution or grant involved, so participants should contact their local administrators if they wish to explore this option. Please note that reimbursements must be made to individuals and cannot be paid directly to institutions or grants.

We recognise that covering expenses upfront can be challenging. While this is a limitation of the COST reimbursement system, we will do everything possible to make the reimbursement process clear, efficient, and quick, so that participants are reimbursed as soon as possible.

If you have any questions or concerns about the financial aspects of attending the course, please do not hesitate to contact us.
